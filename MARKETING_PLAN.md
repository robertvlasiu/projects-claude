## App 1: Pickleball Tracker

### Who they are
- Age 38–65, skews 45–60
- Household income $75k+
- Play 3–5x per week
- Already spend $150–300/month on memberships, paddles, gear
- Live in: Florida, Arizona, California, Texas, the Carolinas
- Platform: iPhone (older demographic = iOS-dominant)

### Where they live online
| Channel | Size | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| r/pickleball | 700k members | Very active, tools get upvoted |
| Facebook groups | 2M+ total | "Pickleball Forum", "Pickleball Players" — massive |
| Instagram #pickleball | 4M+ posts | Micro-influencers easy to reach |
| TikTok | Growing | Less relevant for 45-60 demo |
| Local clubs | 40,000+ clubs in US | Directors share tools with members |

### Launch plan (Week 1–4)

**Week 1: Reddit post**
Post in r/pickleball: "I play 4x a week and got tired of tracking my game in notes — built this"
- Show 2–3 screenshots, mention it's free to start
- Reply to every comment for 48 hours
- Target: 500+ upvotes → 2,000–5,000 downloads in first week
- Cost: $0, time: 2 hours

**Week 2: Facebook outreach**
- Join 10 large Facebook pickleball groups
- Post authentically: "I built a free app for tracking my matches — wanted to share it with the community"
- Don't spam — pick 2–3 groups, post once in each
- Target: 1,000–2,000 downloads
- Cost: $0, time: 3 hours

**Week 3: Paid Facebook/Instagram ads**
Budget: $10–15/day
Targeting: Age 38–65, interest "Pickleball", US only, iOS devices
Creative: Simple screenshot carousel + "Finally an app that takes your game seriously"
- Test 3 ad creatives, keep top performer
- Expected CPI (cost per install): $1.50–3.00
- $10/day → 5–7 installs/day → 150–210/month from ads alone
- Cost: ~$450/month

**Week 4: Club partnerships**
- Email 50 pickleball club directors: "We built a free app your members would love"
- Offer: Free premium codes for the director and their top instructors
- Ask: Share it in their member newsletter / Facebook group
- 50 clubs × 10% response × 50 member shares = ~250 installs
- Cost: $0, time: 4 hours (Claude writes the email)

**Ongoing: App Store optimization (ASO)**
Keywords to target: "pickleball tracker", "pickleball score", "pickleball stats", "paddle sport tracker", "pickleball game log"
- Write a compelling App Store description (Claude writes it)
- Add 10 screenshots showing key screens
- Organic App Store traffic: 20–40% of total downloads long-term

## Pricing psychology

### Pickleball: $6.99/month
- Players pay $150–300/month in gym/club fees
- $6.99 is "less than one can of balls"
- Annual option ($49.99) = "Save 40%" framing — target 30% of subscribers to take this

### Flock: $4.99/month
- Keepers spend $100+/month on feed
- Lower price means less friction, higher conversion
- Annual option ($29.99) should be the default prompt after 7 days

### Upgrade timing
- Show upgrade prompt after user logs their **3rd match / adds their 4th bird**
  (they're invested, they see value, the habit is forming)
- Not on first launch — let them experience the value first
- Offer 3-day free trial of premium after prompt

## What NOT to do

- Don't buy fake reviews. Apple will remove your app.
- Don't post the same message in 20 Facebook groups in one day. You'll be banned.
- Don't run ads before you have 20+ real App Store reviews. Conversion tanks without social proof.
- Don't build a backend in v1. Local-first means no servers, no GDPR headaches, no downtime.
- Don't raise the price after launch without grandfathering existing subscribers.
